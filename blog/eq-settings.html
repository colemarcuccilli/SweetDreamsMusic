The Best EQ Settings for Professional Vocals

Reading time: 20 min Category: Tutorials

Understanding Vocal EQ

EQ is the foundation of professional vocal mixing. At Sweet Dreams Studio, we've developed precise EQ techniques that bring out the best in any voice while solving common problems.

Essential Frequency Ranges

The Complete Vocal Spectrum:

  • Sub-Bass (20-60Hz)
    • Usually unnecessary
    • Cut completely
    • Source of rumble
  • Bass (60-200Hz)
    • Fundamental frequencies
    • Warmth and body
    • Potential mud zone
  • Low-Mids (200-500Hz)
    • Chest resonance
    • Fullness
    • Boxiness zone
  • Mids (500Hz-2kHz)
    • Vocal presence
    • Intelligibility
    • Nasality zone
  • High-Mids (2-7kHz)
    • Clarity and definition
    • Attack and bite
    • Harshness zone
  • Highs (7-20kHz)
    • Air and brilliance
    • Sparkle
    • Sibilance zone

Sweet Dreams Studio Tip:

Always EQ with purpose. Don't make changes just because you think you should - let your ears guide you.

Genre-Specific EQ Settings

Pop Vocals:

  • High-Pass Filter: 100Hz
  • Cut 250Hz: -2dB (reduce mud)
  • Boost 3kHz: +2dB (presence)
  • Boost 12kHz: +1dB (air)

Rock Vocals:

  • High-Pass Filter: 80Hz
  • Cut 300Hz: -3dB (reduce boxiness)
  • Boost 2kHz: +3dB (aggression)
  • Boost 5kHz: +2dB (cut through guitars)

R&B/Soul Vocals:

  • High-Pass Filter: 90Hz
  • Boost 150Hz: +1dB (warmth)
  • Cut 400Hz: -2dB (clarity)
  • Boost 10kHz: +1.5dB (silk)

Problem-Solving EQ Techniques

1. Removing Mud:

  • Identify Problem Area
    • Sweep 200-400Hz
    • Find resonance
    • Cut narrowly
  • Solution
    • Q factor: 2.0-3.0
    • Cut: -2 to -4dB
    • Use dynamic EQ if needed

2. Controlling Harshness:

  • Target Frequencies
    • 2-4kHz range
    • Find offensive frequency
    • Use narrow Q
  • Solution
    • Dynamic EQ preferred
    • Threshold: -12dB
    • Reduction: -2 to -3dB

3. Adding Air:

  • High Shelf
    • Starting point: 10kHz
    • Gentle slope
    • Boost: 1-2dB
  • Considerations
    • Watch for sibilance
    • Use quality EQ
    • Check on multiple systems

Professional Insight:

Use surgical EQ cuts and musical EQ boosts. Cuts can be narrow and precise, while boosts should be wider and gentler.

Advanced EQ Techniques

1. Dynamic EQ:

  • Best Uses
    • Controlling peaks
    • Taming resonances
    • Natural-sounding results
  • Key Settings
    • Fast attack (1-2ms)
    • Medium release (50ms)
    • Moderate ratio (2:1)

2. Mid/Side EQ:

  • Mid Channel
    • Focus on clarity
    • Control low-mids
    • Enhance presence
  • Side Channel
    • Add width
    • Enhance air
    • Control stereo image

3. Linear Phase EQ:

  • Best For
    • Mastering
    • Parallel processing
    • Critical phase alignment
  • Considerations
    • Higher CPU usage
    • Pre-ringing potential
    • Latency compensation

EQ Workflow Tips

Step-by-Step Process:

  1. Listen First
    • Identify issues
    • Note desired improvements
    • Plan approach
  2. Clean Up
    • High-pass filter
    • Remove resonances
    • Control problem areas
  3. Enhance
    • Add presence
    • Boost air
    • Fine-tune balance
  4. Check Context
    • Listen in mix
    • Adjust as needed
    • Verify improvements

Sweet Dreams Studio Secret:

Create different EQ settings for verses and choruses. Subtle changes in EQ can help vocals sit perfectly in each section.

Common EQ Mistakes

  • Over-Processing
    • Too many bands
    • Excessive boosts
    • Unnecessary changes
  • Wrong Approach
    • EQing in solo
    • Copying settings
    • Ignoring context
  • Technical Errors
    • Phase issues
    • Filter resonance
    • Gain staging problems

Final Checklist

  • Clean low end
  • Controlled low-mids
  • Present but not harsh
  • Natural air and brilliance
  • No resonances
  • Sits well in mix
  • Translates across systems
