How to Mix 808s and Bass for a Professional Sound

Reading time: 20 min Category: Tutorials

The Power of 808s in Modern Production

The 808 bass has become the foundation of modern hip-hop and trap music. At Sweet Dreams Studio, we've mixed thousands of tracks, and we'll share our proven techniques for achieving powerful, clean 808s that translate on any system.

Understanding 808 Frequencies

Key Frequency Ranges:

  • Sub Bass (20-60Hz)
    • Foundation and weight
    • System-dependent
    • Careful management needed
  • Low Bass (60-120Hz)
    • Main power zone
    • Translation focus
    • Critical balance point
  • Upper Bass (120-250Hz)
    • Definition and punch
    • Small speaker presence
    • Potential mud zone

Sweet Dreams Studio Tip:

Focus on the 60-120Hz range for 808s that translate well across all systems. The sub frequencies should complement, not dominate.

808 Sample Selection

Key Characteristics:

  • Tonal Quality
    • Clean fundamental
    • Controlled harmonics
    • Consistent sustain
  • Dynamic Range
    • Strong initial attack
    • Smooth decay
    • Appropriate length
  • Character
    • Genre-appropriate tone
    • Distortion content
    • Pitch flexibility

Essential Processing Chain

Step-by-Step Approach:

  • 1. Clean-up
    • High-pass below 20Hz
    • Remove resonances
    • Control mud frequencies
  • 2. Tone Shaping
    • Enhance fundamental
    • Add harmonics
    • Control transients
  • 3. Dynamic Control
    • Consistent level
    • Punch enhancement
    • Sustain management

EQ Techniques for 808s

Strategic EQ Moves:

  • Low End
    • High-pass at 20Hz
    • Boost fundamental (40-60Hz)
    • Control resonances
  • Definition
    • Enhance 100-200Hz
    • Cut mud (200-300Hz)
    • Add presence (1-2kHz)

Saturation and Distortion

Adding Character:

  • Harmonic Enhancement
    • Tube saturation
    • Tape emulation
    • Wave shaping
  • Parallel Processing
    • Clean/dirty blend
    • Multiple stages
    • Frequency splitting

Professional Insight:

Use multiband saturation to add harmonics only where needed, keeping the sub frequencies clean while adding presence to higher frequencies.

Sidechain Techniques

Kick and 808 Relationship:

  • Traditional Sidechain
    • Fast attack (1-5ms)
    • Quick release (50-100ms)
    • Moderate ratio (2:1 - 4:1)
  • Volume Shaping
    • Custom curves
    • Pattern-based ducking
    • Dynamic control

Pattern Programming

Rhythmic Considerations:

  • Note Length
    • Pattern-appropriate sustain
    • Controlled overlaps
    • Space for kick
  • Pitch Slides
    • Musical transitions
    • Tension creation
    • Energy building

Translation Techniques

System Compatibility:

  • Frequency Balance
    • Sub/bass ratio
    • Harmonic content
    • Level management
  • Monitoring Strategy
    • Multiple systems
    • Reference tracks
    • Level matching

Sweet Dreams Studio Secret:

Create a parallel distortion track with heavy processing, high-pass it around 100Hz, and blend it in to ensure your 808s cut through on small speakers.

Common 808 Problems

1. Inconsistent Level:

  • Causes:
    • Poor sample selection
    • Inadequate compression
    • Pattern issues
  • Solutions:
    • Multi-stage compression
    • Pattern adjustment
    • Sample normalization

2. Lack of Translation:

  • Issues:
    • Too much sub
    • Insufficient harmonics
    • Poor monitoring
  • Solutions:
    • Harmonic enhancement
    • Better monitoring
    • Reference comparison
