## RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, the Company operates a professional recording studio and online music platform located in Fort Wayne, Indiana; and

**WHEREAS**, the Company desires to engage the Contractor as an independent contractor to provide audio engineering services; and

**WHEREAS**, the Contractor possesses the skills, experience, and expertise necessary to provide such services and desires to be engaged by the Company on the terms set forth herein;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and agreements contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS

**1.1 Independent Contractor.** The Contractor is engaged as an **independent contractor** and is **not an employee, partner, agent, or joint venturer** of the Company. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employer-employee relationship between the Parties.

**1.2 No Employee Benefits.** Contractor is not entitled to any employee benefits, including but not limited to health insurance, paid time off, retirement benefits, workers' compensation, unemployment insurance, or any other benefits provided by the Company to its employees, if any.

**1.3 Control.** Contractor retains the right to control the manner and means by which engineering services are performed, subject to the quality standards and general guidelines established by the Company. The Company's interest is limited to the results of the Contractor's work.

**1.4 Other Engagements.** Contractor is free to provide services to other clients and entities, subject to the non-compete and confidentiality provisions of this Agreement.

---

## 2. SERVICES AND DUTIES

**2.1 Scope of Services.** Contractor shall provide professional audio engineering services, including but not limited to:

- **(a)** Recording, tracking, and capturing audio during studio sessions.
- **(b)** Mixing and basic mastering of session recordings as requested by clients.
- **(c)** Setting up and configuring studio equipment for recording sessions.
- **(d)** Providing technical guidance and expertise to clients during sessions.
- **(e)** Delivering session files to clients through the Sweet Dreams Music dashboard or other approved delivery methods.
- **(f)** Maintaining a professional and welcoming studio environment during sessions.

**2.2 Session Claiming.** Contractor shall claim available recording sessions through the **Sweet Dreams Music platform** (website/dashboard). The Company is not obligated to guarantee any minimum number of sessions to the Contractor.

**2.3 Session Commitment.** Once a session has been claimed by the Contractor, the Contractor is expected to fulfill that commitment. Failure to appear for a claimed session without adequate notice (at least **24 hours**) may result in disciplinary action, including potential termination of this Agreement.

**2.4 Scheduling.** Contractor sets their own availability and is not required to work any minimum number of hours or sessions. Contractor may decline to claim sessions at their discretion.

**2.5 Platform Compliance.** Contractor agrees to use the Sweet Dreams Music platform for session management, including claiming sessions, tracking session progress, and delivering files to clients in accordance with Platform procedures.

---

## 3. COMPENSATION

**3.1 Revenue Share.** Contractor shall receive **[X]%** of the total session revenue for each session that Contractor works (the "Engineer Share"). The Company shall retain the remaining **[100-X]%** (the "Company Share").

**3.2 Session Revenue.** "Session revenue" is defined as the total amount paid by the client for the recording session, including the base hourly rate and any applicable surcharges (same-day booking surcharge, late night surcharges), but **excluding**:

- **(a)** Payment processing fees charged by the payment processor.
- **(b)** Refunds or chargebacks.
- **(c)** Taxes collected and remitted by the Company.

**3.3 Payout Schedule.** Contractor payouts shall be made on a **[weekly / bi-weekly / monthly]** basis through the Platform's designated payment processor (currently Stripe Connect) or via another mutually agreed-upon payment method.

**3.4 Payout Conditions.** Payouts are subject to:

- **(a)** Successful completion of the session and delivery of files to the client.
- **(b)** No unresolved client complaints or disputes related to the session.
- **(c)** Contractor having a valid and verified payment account linked to the Platform.

**3.5 Disputed Sessions.** If a client disputes a session charge or files a complaint, the Company may withhold the Engineer Share for that session pending resolution of the dispute. If the dispute is resolved in favor of the client (resulting in a refund), the Engineer Share for that session will not be paid.

**3.6 No Minimum Guarantee.** The Company does not guarantee any minimum level of compensation, sessions, or income to the Contractor.

---

## 4. TAXES AND REPORTING

**4.1 Tax Responsibility.** Contractor is **solely responsible** for reporting and paying all applicable federal, state, and local taxes on compensation received under this Agreement, including but not limited to:

- **(a)** Federal and state income taxes.
- **(b)** Self-employment taxes (Social Security and Medicare).
- **(c)** Any other taxes or contributions required by law.

**4.2 No Withholding.** The Company will **not** withhold any taxes from payments to the Contractor. The Company will not make contributions to Social Security, Medicare, federal or state unemployment insurance, or workers' compensation on behalf of the Contractor.

**4.3 IRS Form 1099.** The Company will issue IRS **Form 1099-NEC** (or such other form as may be required by law) to the Contractor for all compensation paid during each calendar year, to the extent required by applicable law.

**4.4 Tax Documentation.** Contractor agrees to provide a completed **IRS Form W-9** (or equivalent) to the Company prior to receiving any compensation. Failure to provide accurate tax documentation may result in a delay of payouts.

**4.5 Indemnification for Taxes.** Contractor agrees to indemnify and hold harmless the Company from and against any liability arising from Contractor's failure to pay applicable taxes, including any penalties, interest, or assessments imposed by taxing authorities.

---

## 5. EQUIPMENT AND FACILITIES

**5.1 Company-Provided Equipment.** The Company shall provide the studio space, recording equipment, software, and facilities necessary for the performance of engineering services, including but not limited to:

- **(a)** Recording console / audio interface.
- **(b)** Microphones, cables, and accessories.
- **(c)** Studio monitors and headphones.
- **(d)** Digital Audio Workstation (DAW) software and plugins installed on studio computers.
- **(e)** Acoustic treatment and studio furniture.

**5.2 Equipment Care.** Contractor shall treat all Company-provided equipment with reasonable care and report any equipment malfunctions, damage, or maintenance needs promptly. Contractor shall not remove any Company equipment from the studio premises without prior written authorization.

**5.3 Contractor's Tools.** Contractor may use personal equipment, software, or plugins during sessions at their discretion, provided such use does not interfere with studio operations or create compatibility issues.

**5.4 Damage Liability.** Contractor shall be financially responsible for any damage to Company equipment or facilities caused by Contractor's negligence or willful misconduct. Normal wear and tear is excluded.

## 6. CONFIDENTIALITY

**6.1 Confidential Information.** Contractor acknowledges that in the course of performing services, Contractor may have access to confidential and proprietary information of the Company and its clients, including but not limited to:

- **(a)** Client personal information (names, contact information, payment details).
- **(b)** Unreleased recordings, session files, and creative works.
- **(c)** Business strategies, pricing, financial data, and operational procedures.
- **(d)** Client lists, booking records, and session histories.
- **(e)** Proprietary technology, systems, and platform information.

**6.2 Non-Disclosure Obligation.** Contractor agrees to keep all Confidential Information **strictly confidential** and shall not disclose, publish, share, or otherwise make available any Confidential Information to any third party, except as required to perform services under this Agreement or as required by law.

**6.3 Session Recordings.** Contractor shall **not** retain, copy, distribute, share, upload, or otherwise disseminate any client session recordings or files, except as necessary to deliver completed files to the client through approved Platform channels.

**6.4 Social Media.** Contractor shall not post, share, or reference any client session details, recordings, or behind-the-scenes content on social media or any public platform without the **express written or electronic consent** of both the client and the Company.

**6.5 Survival.** The obligations under this Section 6 shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement and shall remain in effect **indefinitely** with respect to Confidential Information.

## 7. NON-COMPETE AND NON-SOLICITATION

**7.1 Non-Solicitation of Clients.** During the term of this Agreement and for a period of **[X] months** following termination, Contractor shall **not** directly or indirectly solicit, contact, or attempt to divert any client of Sweet Dreams Music for the purpose of providing competing recording, engineering, or production services outside of the Sweet Dreams Music platform.

**7.2 Scope of Non-Solicitation.** This non-solicitation provision applies only to clients that:

- **(a)** Contractor was introduced to through the Sweet Dreams Music platform; or
- **(b)** Contractor provided services to during sessions at the Sweet Dreams Music studio.

**7.3 Pre-Existing Relationships.** This non-solicitation provision does **not** apply to clients with whom Contractor had a pre-existing professional relationship prior to the Effective Date of this Agreement, provided that Contractor discloses such relationships to the Company in writing.

**7.4 Independent Work.** Contractor is free to operate an independent engineering business and accept clients who are not Sweet Dreams Music clients, provided such work does not violate the confidentiality or non-solicitation provisions of this Agreement.

**7.5 Reasonableness.** The Parties acknowledge that the scope, duration, and geographic limitations of this non-compete/non-solicitation provision are reasonable and necessary to protect the Company's legitimate business interests. If a court of competent jurisdiction finds any provision of this Section to be overbroad or unenforceable, the Parties agree that the court may modify the provision to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable.

## 8. QUALITY STANDARDS AND PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT

**8.1 Professional Standards.** Contractor agrees to perform all services in a **professional, competent, and workmanlike manner** consistent with industry standards for professional audio engineering.

**8.2 Client Experience.** Contractor shall maintain a positive, respectful, and professional demeanor with all clients and guests during sessions. Contractor is a representative of the Sweet Dreams Music brand while working in the studio.

**8.3 Studio Rules.** Contractor shall comply with and enforce all studio rules and policies as established by the Company, including but not limited to rules regarding smoking, weapons, controlled substances, guest limits, and equipment handling.

**8.4 Punctuality.** Contractor is expected to arrive at the studio with sufficient time to set up and prepare before the client's scheduled session start time (generally at least **15 minutes** prior).

**8.5 File Delivery.** Contractor shall deliver all session files to clients through the Sweet Dreams Music dashboard or other approved methods within the timeframe established by Company policy (currently **[X] business days** from the session date).

**8.6 Feedback and Complaints.** If a client submits a complaint regarding the quality of engineering services, the Company will notify the Contractor and work collaboratively to address the issue. Repeated complaints may result in corrective action or termination under Section 9.

---

## 9. TERM AND TERMINATION

**9.1 Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in effect until terminated by either Party in accordance with this Section 9 (the "Term").

**9.2 Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time, for any reason or no reason, by providing **thirty (30) days' written notice** to the other Party.

**9.3 Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement **immediately** upon written notice if the other Party:

- **(a)** Materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within fifteen (15) days after receiving written notice thereof.
- **(b)** Engages in fraud, dishonesty, or criminal conduct.
- **(c)** Engages in conduct that materially harms the reputation of the other Party or the Sweet Dreams Music brand.
- **(d)** Violates the confidentiality, non-solicitation, or studio conduct provisions of this Agreement.

**9.4 Immediate Termination.** The Company may terminate this Agreement immediately without notice or cure period if Contractor:

- **(a)** Engages in threatening, violent, or abusive behavior.
- **(b)** Is found to be under the influence of drugs or alcohol during a session to the extent it impairs their ability to perform services.
- **(c)** Steals, damages, or misappropriates Company property.
- **(d)** Shares or distributes client recordings without authorization.

**9.5 Effects of Termination.** Upon termination of this Agreement:

- **(a)** Contractor shall immediately cease claiming new sessions through the Platform.
- **(b)** Contractor shall complete any sessions already scheduled and confirmed at the time of the termination notice, unless the Company directs otherwise.
- **(c)** Contractor shall deliver all outstanding session files to clients within the established timeframe.
- **(d)** Contractor shall return any Company property in their possession.
- **(e)** Any outstanding compensation owed to Contractor for completed sessions shall be paid within thirty (30) days of the effective termination date.
- **(f)** The provisions of Sections 1, 4, 6, 7, 8, 10, and 12 shall survive termination.

## 10. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

**10.1 Client Ownership.** All recordings, mixes, and creative works produced during studio sessions belong to the **client** who booked and paid for the session. Neither the Company nor the Contractor claims ownership over client-created content.

**10.2 Work-for-Hire.** To the extent that Contractor's engineering services contribute to copyrightable works during a session, such contributions are considered a **work-for-hire** for the benefit of the client. If any work is deemed not to be a work-for-hire, Contractor hereby assigns all rights, title, and interest in such work to the client.

**10.3 Promotional Use.** The Company retains the right to use session-related content (photos, short clips, references) for promotional purposes **only with the client's prior consent**. Contractor may also reference completed work in their personal portfolio with the prior consent of both the client and the Company.

**10.4 Company IP.** Contractor acknowledges that the Sweet Dreams Music name, logo, branding, website, platform, and all related intellectual property are the exclusive property of Sweet Dreams LLC. Contractor shall not use Company IP for personal purposes without written authorization.
